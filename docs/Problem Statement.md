## Problem Statement

> **"Use historical weather, soil, and satellite data to develop ML models predicting crop yield, helping farmers make informed planting decisions."**[^1]

***

## Background \& Context

Agriculture is the backbone of India's economy, yet farmers constantly face uncertainty in crop planning due to unpredictable weather, variable soil conditions, and lack of data-driven tools. Traditional methods of predicting crop yield rely on experience or trial-and-error, which frequently result in suboptimal crop selection, lower yields, and financial losses. The integration of historical weather records, soil properties, and satellite imagery (e.g., NDVI from Sentinel-2) with machine learning provides an opportunity to build accurate, scalable prediction systems.[^2][^3]

***

## Core Objective

Build a machine learning model or system that:

- Ingests **historical weather data** (temperature, rainfall, humidity, dew point)
- Uses **soil data** (nutrients, moisture, type, pH)
- Leverages **satellite imagery/data** (e.g., NDVI, MODIS, Landsat, Sentinel-2)
- Outputs **crop yield predictions** to support farmers in choosing what, when, and where to plant[^4][^2]

***

## Expected Solution Features

- **Data pipeline** to collect and preprocess multi-source data (weather, soil, satellite)
- **ML model(s)** — candidates include Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM, SVR, or deep learning (LSTM/CNN for time-series satellite data)[^5][^6]
- **Yield prediction output** — estimated yield for a given crop in a given region/season
- **Crop recommendation** — suggest the most suitable crop based on current conditions[^3]
- **Farmer-friendly interface** — dashboard or app presenting actionable insights in simple language[^2]

***

## Key Data Sources to Consider

| Data Type | Examples |
| :-- | :-- |
| Weather | Temperature, rainfall, humidity, wind speed (IMD, ERA5, Open-Meteo) |
| Soil | NPK levels, pH, moisture, soil type (NBSS\&LUP, FAO SoilGrids) |
| Satellite | NDVI, EVI from Sentinel-2, Landsat, MODIS [^4][^2] |
| Historical Yield | Crop production data from agriculture ministry/Kaggle datasets |


***

## Problem Constraints / Challenges

- Integrating **heterogeneous, multi-modal data** sources with different resolutions and formats[^4]
- Ensuring the model works for **regional/small-scale farms**, not just country-level aggregates[^2]
- Handling **missing or noisy data** from historical records
- Balancing model accuracy with **real-time or near-real-time** performance for practical use[^6]

***

## Judging Criteria (Typical for such Hackathons)

- Accuracy and reliability of yield prediction
- Quality and diversity of data sources used
- Innovation in the ML approach
- Usability of the final product for farmers
- Scalability and deployment feasibility

The hackathon runs for **24 hours** at UVPCE's New Building, Lab 104, as part of **CONVERGENCE 2026** — Ganpat University's annual tech fest themed **"AI and Ethics"**.[^1]
